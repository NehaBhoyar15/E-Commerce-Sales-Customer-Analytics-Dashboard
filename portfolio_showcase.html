SQL Analysis Highlights

-- Master KPI Summary Query
SELECT
    ROUND(SUM(sales),  2)                                AS total_revenue,
    ROUND(SUM(profit), 2)                                AS total_profit,
    ROUND(SUM(profit) / NULLIF(SUM(sales),0) * 100, 2)  AS profit_margin_pct,
    COUNT(DISTINCT order_id)                             AS total_orders,
    COUNT(DISTINCT customer_id)                          AS total_customers,
    ROUND(SUM(sales)/NULLIF(COUNT(DISTINCT order_id),0), 2) AS avg_order_value,
    ROUND(AVG(DATEDIFF(ship_date, order_date)), 1)       AS avg_shipping_days
FROM superstore_sales;
-- Top 10 Customers by Revenue
SELECT
    customer_name, segment, region,
    COUNT(DISTINCT order_id)                     AS total_orders,
    ROUND(SUM(sales),  2)                        AS total_revenue,
    ROUND(SUM(profit), 2)                        AS total_profit,
    ROUND(SUM(profit)/NULLIF(SUM(sales),0)*100,2) AS profit_margin_pct
FROM superstore_sales
GROUP BY customer_name, segment, region
ORDER BY total_revenue DESC
LIMIT 10;
-- Monthly Revenue with MoM Growth %
SELECT
    DATE_FORMAT(order_date,'%b %Y') AS month_label,
    ROUND(SUM(sales), 2)            AS monthly_revenue,
    ROUND(
      (SUM(sales) - LAG(SUM(sales)) OVER (ORDER BY YEAR(order_date), MONTH(order_date)))
      / NULLIF(LAG(SUM(sales)) OVER (ORDER BY YEAR(order_date), MONTH(order_date)),0)*100
    , 2)                            AS mom_growth_pct
FROM superstore_sales
GROUP BY YEAR(order_date), MONTH(order_date)
ORDER BY YEAR(order_date), MONTH(order_date);
-- Discount Band Impact on Profitability
SELECT
    CASE
        WHEN discount = 0      THEN 'No Discount'
        WHEN discount <= 0.10  THEN '1–10%'
        WHEN discount <= 0.20  THEN '11–20%'
        WHEN discount <= 0.30  THEN '21–30%'
        ELSE '31%+'
    END                              AS discount_band,
    COUNT(*)                         AS order_lines,
    ROUND(AVG(profit), 2)            AS avg_profit,
    ROUND(SUM(profit)/NULLIF(SUM(sales),0)*100, 2) AS margin_pct
FROM superstore_sales
GROUP BY discount_band
ORDER BY avg_profit DESC;

Key Business Insights

📉

Discounts >20% Generate Losses

Discount–Profit correlation: −0.22. Orders with 30%+ discounts average a −15% profit margin. Recommend a 15% discount cap policy.

🏆

Top 23% Customers = 47% Revenue

Classic Pareto distribution confirmed. Champion customers drive nearly half of all revenue. Retention investment here has the highest ROI.

📅

Q4 Peaks 35% Above Annual Avg

Holiday-driven spike is consistent across all 4 years. Pre-stock inventory and launch campaigns by September to maximise Q4 capture.

🪑

Tables & Bookcases Are Loss-Makers

Tables: −$17.7K profit despite $206K in sales. Heavy discounting erodes margins. Urgent pricing audit needed for Furniture sub-categories.

🗺️

Central Region Underperforms

Central has the lowest profit margin (8.3%) vs West's 16.8%. Root cause: aggressive Furniture discounting in Midwestern markets.

📄

Paper Has the Best Margin (43.6%)

Office Supplies — Paper sub-category generates a 43.6% margin with minimal discounting. An expansion strategy here could lift overall margins.
